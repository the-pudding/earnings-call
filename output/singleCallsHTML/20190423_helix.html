In the first quarter, the Siem Helix 1 achieved 98% utilization, working on three wells undertaking production enhancement works. The Siem Helix 2 achieved 98% utilization working on five wells performing production enhancement works.

Moving onto Slide 14 for our robotics review, Q1 2019 year-over-year was a much improved quarter for robotics with strong operational performance and much improved commercial utilization. In the first quarter, vessel charter fleet utilization was 88% with two vessels utilized mostly on renewables trenching projects in the North Sea and one vessel contracted on two ROV support projects in the Gulf of Mexico. We also contracted 84 days of project-oriented spot market vessel utilization.

The Grand Canyon worked in the North Sea achieving a 100% utilization on a hard ground trenching project, utilizing our high performance hard ground trencher, a jet trencher and two support ROVs. The Grand Canyon II located in the Gulf of Mexico had 72% utilization on two deepwater ROV support scopes. The Grand Canyon III had 80% utilization, mostly performing works on trenching projects and ROV support works in the North Sea.

Our outlook for 2019 robotics is currently improved with our loan cost improvements and we've already secured meaningful utilization, and we continue to add more contracts to the fleets working in both trenched and ROV support. Our outlook remains confident in improving the EBITDA over the next couple of years. We'll continue to pay down our remaining debt and we'll control the capital spending. Once the final payment is made on the Q7000 this year, we look forward to a strong free cash flow generation. Going forward, we're proceeding consistent with the Company's long-standing succession plan. You can look for an announcement here in the next week or so and we expect nothing but a smooth transition.
