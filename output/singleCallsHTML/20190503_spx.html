Thank you, Bella, and good morning, everyone. Thank you for joining us. With me here today are Marc Michael, president and ceo; and Jaime Easley, our chief financial officer. Our Q1 2019 earnings release was issued this morning and can be found on our website, spxflow.com.

Additionally, in a separate news release, we announced our decision to narrow our strategy toward building a premier process solutions enterprise. Marc Michael -- President and Chief Executive Officer

Thanks, Ryan. Good morning, everyone, and thanks for joining us on this very exciting and significant day for our team at SPX FLOW. Over the past few years, we have made great progress on our journey to transform SPX FLOW into a high-performing operating enterprise, and I'm proud of our teams across the company for their efforts on this front. As we discussed on our Q4 earnings call, the journey to high performance continues in 2019 as our global team is aligned on the key initiatives we're emphasizing to drive a higher quality of revenue, continuous operating improvement and a world-class customer experience.

Our Q1 results reflect continued progress on these initiatives. We delivered solid growth over the prior year, with adjusted EBITDA up 22%, organic revenue growth of 5% and adjusted operating margin up 170 points. Our Q1 results exceeded our guidance expectations on the back of strong operational execution. With a strong start to the year, we have reaffirmed our full year guidance.

I'm proud of our teams across the world for their contributions to our first-quarter results. In addition to the solid operating performance in Q1, we've been actively working to accelerate our strategic initiatives. On that front, today, we announced the next step on our journey to high performance. After a thorough strategic review of our product lines, core competencies and customer segments, we determined that the value proposition to customers in process applications differs from those in flow control applications.

To best serve customers in both these areas, we have chosen to narrow our strategic focus on process solutions and to pursue a divestiture of power and energy. We're very excited about this strategy and believe these actions will unlock significant value for shareholders and position our teams in both businesses for future success. We'll discuss this strategy in detail later on the call. Before we dive into that discussion, Jaime and I will provide a high-level recap of our Q1 performance.

In the first quarter, we delivered 5% organic revenue growth, led by our food and beverage and industrial segments. On an adjusted basis, operating income was $42 million, exceeding our guidance range, with operating margins up 170 points year over year. Adjusted EBITDA grew 22% to $54 million. Adjusted EBITDA margins improved 190 points to 11%, marking our seventh consecutive quarter of year-over-year margin expansion.

And we generated $15 million of free cash flow, a good start to the year as compared to our historical seasonality. On the order front, we experienced a slow start to the year, with organic orders down 5% versus Q1 2018. The year-over-year decline was concentrated in long-cycle, customer capex-oriented orders in power and energy, and to a lesser extent, food and beverage. On a positive note, industrial orders grew low single digits over the prior year, led by our mixer and hydraulic tools product lines.

Notably, we saw a pick-up in orders across all three segments during March, giving us positive momentum into Q2. Looking more closely at organic orders through the lens of our product line investment roles. In our aggressive growth products, organic orders declined modestly versus the prior year due primarily to lower demand for industrial pumps used in fracking and general industrial applications. This decline was largely offset by solid order growth in mixers and hydraulic tools.

In the opportunistic and enhance categories, as compared to the prior year, we booked a lower level of midstream pumps and valve orders in power and energy, and a lower level of system orders and long-term service agreements in food and beverage. Quarterly comparisons in these product lines tend to be lumpy. We're not overly concerned with the Q1 order levels given the healthy frontlog activity our teams are seeing from customers. Specifically, strong quoting activity for pipeline valves in North America and for liquid process systems in Asia-Pacific.

We anticipate orders in these areas to pick up as the year progresses. It is important to view our product -- excuse me, it is important to view our order and backlog development in context of our strategy to drive a higher quality of revenue, expand gross margins and reduce the risk profile inherent in large projects. Overall, we continue to execute toward these goals through disciplined pricing initiatives and selectivity on large projects. Our pricing initiatives have been well executed year to date, and I'm pleased with the improvement in our processes across the board on this front.

Backlog margins continue to improve, a positive trend that we expect to contribute to the favorable mix shift anticipated in our revenue and margin guidance for the second half. Sequentially, organic orders in our Aggressive Growth category increased 3% with good momentum across the majority of the product lines. On a trailing 12-month basis, our aggressive growth product orders are up 4% year over year, a good indicator of the long-term organic growth profile in these products. To support future growth, Ty Jeffers is aligning talent, investments and operating initiatives at the key factories that deliver these products.

Over the last nine months, we've added talent to our global manufacturing team with an emphasis on strong site leadership and continuous improvement capability. We have a strong team in the field, and we are counting on to help us increase our velocity and throughput to win operationally going forward. At this time, I'll turn the call over to Jaime.

Jaime Easley -- Chief Financial Officer

Thanks, Marc, and good morning, everyone. I'll begin with earnings per share. On an adjusted basis, Q1 EPS was $0.44 per share, up 22% from the prior year and exceeded our guidance range. As compared to our guidance, segment income was $0.12 benefit, concentrated in our industrial and food and beverage segments.

Overall, our teams across all three segments delivered solid operating execution in the period. Taxes were a $0.04 headwind as the geographical mix of earnings drove an effective tax rate of $0.34 versus our guidance assumption of 29%. On a GAAP basis, we reported earnings per share of $0.46. This included the following items: in other income, we recorded a mark-to-market gain of $0.10 related to an equity investment in an asset divested prior to our spin.

The benefit was recorded in conjunction with the prior year adoption of a new accounting standard regarding the treatment of equity securities. Under this new standard, we are required to record a mark-to-market adjustment on this investment on a quarterly basis going forward. The benefit of this gain was offset partially by $0.07 of restructuring expense related to the strategic actions we announced on our Q4 call to reduce our exposure to large drying applications in food and beverage. Additionally, professional fees associated with the strategic actions we announced this morning totaled approximately $0.01 of EPS.

Looking at our consolidated revenue and segment income. Revenue was $491 million, flat to the prior year as 5% organic growth was offset by currency headwinds. Segment income was $55 million, up 10% year over year, and margins expanded 100 points to 11.3%. Moving on to the segment results, beginning with food and beverage.

Q1 revenue was $173 million, up 4% from the prior year. Currency was a 5% headwind. Organic growth was 9% driven by execution on large dry orders booked in 2017, and to a lesser extent, increased component sales. Segment income was $19 million and margins were 10.7%.

The low incremental margins on the organic revenue growth reflect the margin profile in drying applications. Organic orders were down 6%. This decline was concentrated in North America as we saw a lower level of component demand from channel partners and end users as compared to our Q1 2018 when demand was fairly strong. Overall, the market trends in food and beverage remain healthy.

We view this market as a steady growth market over time. Dwight Gibson and his team continue to focus on growing our components, service and spare parts revenue streams, while remaining selective and disciplined on systems opportunities. Taking a look at power and energy. Q1 revenue was $136 million, down 6% year over year.

Currency was a 4% headwind and organic revenue declined 2%. The organic decline reflects a lower level of OE shipments and replacement pumps. Segment income was $10 million and margins declined 120 points to 7.2%, reflecting an unfavorable revenue mix as compared to the prior year. Organic revenues declined 16%, primarily due to a lower level of midstream valve and pump orders, particularly in North America.

To a lesser extent, aftermarket orders also declined year over year. As Marc noted, we are seeing positive quote and bid activity in Q2 related largely to North American midstream opportunities. And we believe we are positioned to win in this market. Overall, we believe oil and gas markets are healthy and anticipate a higher level of quarterly orders as the year progresses, and we remain focused on committed to serving our customers at a high level through this strategic transformation.

Wrapping up the segment results with industrial. Q1 revenue was $182 million, up 2% year over year. Currency was a 4% headwind. Organic growth of 6% was led by our mixer, dehydration and hydraulic product lines.

Segment income was $27 million, up 32% year over year. And margins expanded 350 points to 14.9%. The improved profitability was largely driven by leverage on the organic growth. Also note that last year's results included a $3 million repair charge on a large mixer in China.

On that note, I want to highlight the work our mixer team did in responding to that customer issue, designing an alternative solution and staying close to the customer throughout the process to ensure a successful outcome. Organic orders grew 2%, driven by strong double-digit growth in mixers and heat exchangers, and mid-single-digit order growth for hydraulic tools. This growth more than offset the decline in pump orders. José has built a strong, talented commercial team in our industrial segment that has driven high-quality growth across the product lines.

As this team transitioned to Dwight's organization, we see ample opportunity for further growth and margin expansion. Taking a brief look at backlog. Q1 ending backlog was $917 million. The decline in backlog from prior period reflects the impact of our product line strategy, as well as the 5% organic growth we've delivered in consecutive quarters.

As we executed the large orders in backlog and remain selective on new opportunities, the quality of our backlog continues to improve and the mix is increasingly comprised of high-value component orders. Naturally, this approach is yielding a higher quality revenue profile, which we expect to materialize on our gross margin more prominently in the second half. It is also moving our business profile more heavily toward a short-cycle profile. Moving on to the guidance.

We are reaffirming our full year guidance this morning. Looking at our expectations for the second quarter. Broadly speaking, we expect Q2 to be very similar to Q1. We are targeting approximately $500 million of revenue and $55 million of segment income.

Note that approximately 80% of the revenue target was in our beginning Q2 backlog. Our EPS and EBITDA guidance is on an adjusted basis, and excludes restructuring expense and fees associated with the strategic actions. On this basis, we are targeting EPS between $0.41 and $0.53 per share, which assumes a 28% to 30% tax rate. And we expect EBITDA of about $55 million or approximately 11% of revenue.

As we move into the second half, we expect a higher quality revenue mix, benefits from pricing and restructuring savings to be the key drivers of approximately 250 points of margin expansion over the first half. On this next slide, you'll see an overview of our capital -- our current capital structure. At the end of Q1, we had $202 million of cash on hand and gross debt of $756 million, down modestly from year-end. Net leverage was 2.1x.

And debt-to-capital -- total capitalization was 44%. About 80% of our outstanding debt was at -- is at attractive fixed rates, with just over 20% floating debt, which has interest rates at approximately 4.25%. On the right side, you'll see our debt instruments and maturity dates. We have two $300 million tranches of senior notes that mature in 2024 and 2026.

Our term loan is now at $135 million and matures in Q3 2020. And we have $450 million of capacity in our revolver, which is essentially undrawn at this time. During 2019, we intend to monitor the refinancing markets and will opportunistically look to refinance our credit facility. As a reminder, our full year free cash flow guidance is $105 million to $125 million, and we intend to use a portion of that cash to reduce debt by $50 million to $75 million.

Between cash on hand and our revolver, we have over $600 million of liquidity today. And we will consider the potential proceeds from power and energy divestiture, and we will be in a very strong financial position. As we consider our options for deploying those proceeds based on current information, our intention would be to allocate it to a mix of organic growth investments, further deleveraging and return to shareholders. In doing so, we intend to reset our financial position with ratios and actions that strengthen our ratings profile.

Specifically, we are revising our target leverage -- net leverage guidance to 1.5 to -- or 1.5 times to 2.5 times, a half a turn lower than our previous target. Following the divestiture, we are likely to position net leverage at or near the bottom of that range. As a guiding principle, I believe it is prudent to lead a conservative financial strategy focused on stability and flexibility to ensure we maintain a strong balance sheet and invest for growth in our business through economic cycles. That said, one of my priorities is to improve our capital efficiency, drive our teams to ramp up high-return growth investments above our weighted average cost of capital that create long-term value.

We will maintain discipline in our approach to growth investments and acquisition opportunities, comparing those opportunities against the value of returning capital to shareholders. On that front, we are open to share repurchases and dividends if we do not have a higher-value organic and -- acquisition investment opportunities. In summary, we'll prudently invest capital in areas of opportunity to provide strong ROIC and create long-term value for shareholders consistent with the strategy we announced this morning. At this time, I'll turn the call back over to Marc.

Marc Michael -- President and Chief Executive Officer

Thanks, Jaime. Overall, it was a solid quarter that in many ways, reflects the improvement in our operational performance across the business. Now we'll dive into the details of our strategic transformation. Starting with an overview of the actions we are taking.

First, we are executing a comprehensive plan to narrow our strategic focus on building a premier process solutions enterprise on the foundation of our current food and beverage, industrial and Bran+Luebbe product lines. In conjunction with this decision and with strong support from our board of directors, we are pursuing a sale of our power and energy business. That process is under way. We made this decision after conducting a thorough review of our product portfolio, and we believe it is a logical next step for us.

We're confident in this strategic transformation is the best way to serve customers and maximize future potential, and we are ready to take our business to the next level to deliver a world-class customer experience, elevate our operational excellence, strengthen our balance sheet and add significant capacity for future value creation. Digging a bit deeper on the strategic rationale. Today, we play in two main categories: Process equipment and flow controls. Both require a high degree of engineering and precision to meet customer specifications.

However, there are specific capabilities unique to each category that drive a winning outcome with customers. In process applications, we work closely with customers to engineer and design technologies that combine specific ingredients to create new elements with desired outcomes, such as texture, taste, viscosity and nutrient composition. These technologies perform specific tasks, such as mixing, blending or separating and are integral to our customers' processes. In many cases, our applications engineers are assisting customers to test new recipes for foods, beverages, medicines, skin creams, chemical compounds and many other elements essential to our daily lives.

In FLOW control applications, we work with customers to design or configure equipment to regulate the pressure and flow rate as liquid moves from one point to another, often for a large volume of fluid across long distances, and in some cases, for mission-critical safety measures. We've determined the best way to maximize our ability to serve our customers in both these categories is to separate these parts of our business. In turn, we are confident that this will unlock significant value for shareholders. Looking at power and energy.

This business has approximately $500 million in annual revenue and over 1,800 employees operating in more than 120 countries. It is a global leader in mission-critical, severe-duty pumps and valves, filtration products, and aftermarket parts and services for energy and power customers. And it has a leading position in the midstream, nuclear and power end markets. As for our portfolio is represented by brands that have over 100 years of experience and are recognized for quality, reliability, lead time and engineering expertise in flow-controlled applications.

Jose Larios has been chosen as President of power and energy, and will lead a strong management team with an average of more than 20 years of experience in the flow-controlled, and energy sector. This is a high-quality asset with blue-chip customers. That said, the business profile and end market exposure do not fit with our process solutions strategy. We're confident that pursuing a sale of power and energy is the best path to providing the business and its talented employees with a better opportunity to achieve greater success.

By divesting power and energy, we will reduce our exposure to cyclical energy markets and devote our full attention on building a premier process solutions enterprise. In parallel with the divestiture process, we are tailoring our organization to best serve our customers in process applications, with a goal of delivering a world-class customer experience. This includes 2% to 3% margin expansion we expect to deliver by addressing overhead costs and streamlining functional support. Dwight Gibson will take on a broader role in leading one combined commercial team, with increased focus on utilizing innovative customer and market analytics to drive growth and new product development.

He will work closely with Ty Jeffers, our VP of global manufacturing and supply chain, to optimize and leverage our pathway to excellence lean approach to increase speed and efficiency of customer fulfillment. Over the next 12 to 18 months, we expect the actions we are executing to create a higher quality revenue and gross margin profile. We are targeting a midteens EBITDA margin and a double-digit ROIC profile in the short-term, and we believe this set of products has an organic growth potential above GDP in the 4% to 6% range. Over the medium and long-term, we believe there is potential to achieve a higher margin and ROIC profile through organic growth and continuous operational improvements.

In terms of the proceeds, at this time, our intentions are target organic investments, further deleveraging and returns to shareholders. This will further strengthen our balance sheet and provide ample financial flexibility to invest for growth and create value through disciplined ROIC-centered capital allocation going forward. Process Solutions has an annual revenue of approximately $1.6 billion and is comprised of our food and beverage and industrial segments and our Bran+Luebbe metering pump product line. In these product lines, process expertise and technical engineering capability are at the core of our value propositions for customers in complex, highly regulated applications.

In these applications, our process technology and components are integral to the specific product being treated through our customers' processes. This provides us great customer intimacy and positions us to be a life-cycle service partner to customers, working with them to maximize operating efficiency and uptime, while also meeting regulatory, sustainability and cost of ownership objectives. To support customers, we have a global manufacturing organization with innovation labs and testing centers across all 3 major geographic regions. To further develop these differentiating skill sets, we will focus on talent development, deeper customer intimacy and speed of delivery.

Today, we serve the sanitary industrial markets with a concentration in food and beverage, chemical, pharmaceutical and general industrial markets. We intend to leverage our deep application knowledge, technical engineering and process expertise to expand our customer base and concentrate sales in high-quality process applications with reoccurring revenue streams. In closing, I would like to thank our team members across the enterprise for their contributions to the progress we made over the last three years to improve our operating performance and deliver to our customers on time, on quality and on cost. This strategic transformation is a logical next step for our teams, building on the foundation we've put in place over the last three years.

As we execute this strategy, we remain committed to fully supporting all customers and delivering on our operational improvement objectives. I'm confident these actions will unlock significant underlying value for our shareholders and position both businesses to fully realize their potential. That concludes our prepared remarks. Thank you for joining us on the call today.

And at this time, we'll open it up for questions.
