Adjusting for the favorable impact of the security acquisitions completed in the second quarter of 2018 and the unfavorable impacts of lower average copper prices and generally weak foreign currencies, organic sales increased by 8%. Sales growth was above our outlook range of 3% to 5%, driven by the NSS segment with strong growth in global accounts and Security and Utility customers in our UPS segment. And adjusted earnings per diluted share increased 15% from prior year to $1.33. Let me now review our sales trend by segment, beginning with Network & Security Solutions.

It was driven by global accounts, complex integrated supply programs and our security, wireless and professional audio/video businesses.

We had strong growth with global technology companies and with service providers in the emerging markets. and Ireland due to uncertainty surrounding Brexit and lower first-quarter project activity in the Middle East. Turning to the security slide of the NSS business.

The first-quarter sales of $566 million were flat.

The OEM softness was primarily in the semiconductor and industrial manufacturing verticals.

In EMEA, EES sales of $59 million declined 10% on an organic basis. We saw a declining -- we saw declines in both C&I and OEM businesses in Europe and the Middle East. and Canada as reflected in our bookings and backlog. Let me turn to gross margin on Slide 9 of our presentation. first-quarter gross profit improved 9% to $419 million resulting in gross margin of 19.9%, an increase of 30 basis points year-over-year. PMI remains in expansion territory, and capex spending remains solid, although growth trends have decelerated in recent months. Based on our strong first quarter, the generally solid trends we're experiencing, and the success of our focused sales initiatives tempered by ongoing uncertainties in the external environment, we are increasing our outlook for the full-year 2019 organic sales growth to the 4% to 6.5% range.
