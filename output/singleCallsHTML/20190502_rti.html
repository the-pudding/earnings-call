RTI Surgical Holdings, Inc. (RTIX) Q1 2019 Earnings Call Transcript

RTIX earnings call for the period ending March 31, 2019.

Motley Fool Transcription
(MFTranscription)
May 2, 2019 at 1:31PM
Health Care

Image source: The Motley Fool.

RTI Surgical Holdings, Inc. (NASDAQ:RTIX)
Q1 2019 Earnings Call
May 2, 2019, 8:00 a.m. ET

Contents:

  • Prepared Remarks
  • Questions and Answers
  • Call Participants

Prepared Remarks:

Operator

Good day, ladies and gentlemen, and thank you for standing by. We welcome you to the RTI Surgical First Quarter 2019 Earnings Call.All participants will be on a listen-only mode until the question and answer session. If you would like to ask a question during that time, simply press * then 1 on your telephone keypad. If you would like to withdraw your question, press the # key on your telephone keypad. As a reminder, this conference call may be recorded.

I would now like to turn the conference over to Molly Poarch. Please go ahead.

Molly Poarch -- Investment Relations Officer

Good morning and thank you for joining the call to discuss RTI's first quarter 2019 results. On the call today are Camille Farhat, our President and Chief Executive Officer; and Jonathon Singer, our Chief Financial and Administrative Officer. Camille will provide a brief strategic update and discuss the quarter. Jon will then discuss the first quarter financial results. After the comments, the team will be available to take questions.

Before we start, let me make the following disclosure. The earnings and other matters we will be discussing on this conference call will involve statements that are forward-looking. These statements are based on our management's current expectations, but they are subject to various risks and uncertainties associated with our lines of business and with the economic environment in general. Our actual results may vary from any statements concerning our expectations, about future events that are made during this call. We make no guarantees as to the accuracy of these statements. Accordingly, we urge you to consider all the information about the Company and not to place undue reliance on these forward-looking statements.

During the call, we will also present certain financial information on a non-GAAP basis. Management believes that non-GAAP financial measures taken in conjunction with U.S. GAAP financial measures, provide useful information for both management and investors, by excluding certain non-cash and other expenses that are not indicative of our core operating results.

Management uses non-GAAP measures to compare our performance relative to forecast and strategic plans, to benchmark our performance externally against competitors and for certain compensation decisions. Reconciliations between U.S. GAAP and non-GAAP results are presented in tables accompanying our earnings release, which can be found in the Investor Relations section of our website.

Now I will turn the call over to Camille. Please go ahead.

Camille Farhat -- President and Chief Executive Officer

Good morning, everyone, and thank you for joining us today. 2019 is off to a positive start. We successfully closed the acquisition of Paradigm Spine adding the coflex device to our growing and increasingly differentiated spine portfolio.

In the first quarter, we delivered revenue of approximately $69.7 million with an adjusted EBITDA of $6.6 million or 10% of revenue. Our performance was driven by the increased strength of our Spine portfolio including $2 million from the acquisition of Paradigm as well as a solid performance across our businesses. RTI's performance in the first quarter reflects the strategic and operational progress made toward reducing complexity, driving operational excellence, and accelerating the growth of the company.

Looking ahead, we will continue to explore opportunities to simply our channels, to further anchor our business in the areas where we can build scale and relevance, and drive growth. Our operational excellence initiatives contributed to an eye-popping improvement for the quarter with a gross profit margin of 54.5% compared to 48.2% the prior year. We remain on track to deliver the balance of the anticipated cost savings in 2019.

In accelerating RTI's growth, our Spine franchise is a primary focus including strengthening our portfolio and commercial structure. To that end, one of the key highlights from the quarter was the closing of Paradigm Spine. Many Paradigm team members joined RTI immediately following the close of the acquisition to drive our business forward. Collaboration between the teams has been excellent. Integration continues to progress smoothly and in line with our expectations.

Looking ahead, we're focused on accelerating the growth of coflex through physician engagement, accelerated reimbursement, and sales execution and focus. In an effort to more effectively execute on our goal to drive growth in our Spine business and in conjunction with the integration of Paradigm, we have expanded our US Spine commercial team to focus on two different portfolio areas; novel and established therapies. This approach purposefully aligns products with similar dynamics to our commercial resources and capabilities to drive growth for our Spine business.

Our novel therapies, including coflex, require additional focus on investments and demand generation, expanded surgeon access and training, clinical evaluation, and reimbursement. While our established therapies, which include Fortilink TETRAfuse, ViBone, and our portfolio of hardware, inner body, and ortho-biologic options, require a focus on channel management, distributor development programs, and pricing and contracting strategies. To drive these changes in our overall strategy in Spine forward, we welcome Tom Popeck as the new leader of our Spine franchise.

Tom brings significant commercial leadership experience in the orthopedics industry. He joins us from Johnson & Johnson where he led the commercial strategy for its DePuy Synthes business unit. Prior to J&J, Tom spent 11 years at Stryker Orthopaedics where he led various leadership positions including leading the Stryker Foot and Ankle business unit.

From a global perspective, we are currently in the process of integrating the international Paradigm business. The addition of Paradigm product offering will add approximately 35% more revenue to our international franchise and expands our commercial team. The integration supports our recent switch from distributor to direct sales in Germany, the key target market and significantly increases our customer base there.

Turning to update on the rest of the business. We continue to invest in predictable execution in our Sports and OEM franchises. Within the Sports franchise, we were pleased to see a return to overall growth driven by strong demand for tendons and tissue. The team has been focusing on strengthening our channel partnerships to enhance physician access, training, and demand generation. As we progress through the year, we will continue to evaluate our channel strategy to optimize the opportunities for our tissue-based products. The OEM business performed in line with expectations for first quarter 2019 with a continued focus on extending long-term customer contracts, targeting product development for the product line and geographic expansions, and investing in new platforms of growth that leverage our existing capabilities. The predictable revenue performance of these franchises in conjunction with operating excellence progression provide reliable EBITDA contribution to support our ongoing investment to accelerated growth.

Overall, we are pleased with our strong start for the year and remain focused on executing against our key strategic and operational objectives over the balance of the year.

With that, I will hand over to Jon to outline our financial performance.

Jonathon Singer -- Chief Financial Officer

Thank you, Camille. Jumping right in at the top, RTI's worldwide revenue for the first quarter of 2019 was $69.7 million compared with $69.9 million during the same period for the prior year. Driving 2019 revenues is approximately $0.7 million of net growth within Spine and $300 thousand of growth in Sports offset by declines in OEM. The majority of the decline in OEM is due to $1.1 million of revenue booked in the first quarter of 2018 which was shifted from the second quarter if you recall our discussions of last year.

Spine growth represents $1.4 million contributions from the addition of coflex, $1.3 million net contributions from established Spine growth offset by a $2 million net decline from the suspension of map3. If adjusted for the impact of map3, established Spine products grew approximately 9%. Revenue from the Paradigm acquisition will be recognized in the Spine and the International franchises in a total of approximately $2 million for the quarter as anticipated.

Gross profit for the first quarter of 2019 was $38 million, or 54.5% of revenues, a significant increase compared to $33.7 million, or 48.2% of revenues, in the first quarter of 2018. Gross profit for the first quarter of 2018 was impacted by an inventory charge of $1 million from the write-off of inventory related to our international restructuring and $0.2 million due to the purchase accounting step-up of Zyga inventory.

Marketing general and administrative expenses were $31.9 million for the first quarter of 2019, an increase of $3.5 million or 12.3% compared to $28.4 million in 2018. The increase is predominately driven by the addition of Paradigm and increased legal costs related to patent litigation.

Research and development expenses were $4.3 million in the first quarter of 2019 compared to $3.4 million in 2018. The increase in R&D is driven by the acceleration of organic growth investments. This is a good thing. During the first quarter of 2019, we incurred $9 million of non-recurring acquisition and integration costs to support the acquisition of Paradigm Spine. During the first quarter of 2018, the company incurred $1.8 million of non-recurring charges.

Adjusted earnings before interest, taxes, depreciation, and amortization for the first quarter of 2019 was $6.6 million or 10% of revenue compared with $7.8 million or 11% of revenue for the first quarter of 2018. The decline in adjusted EBITDA is due to increased patent litigation cost and operating expenses focused on accelerating growth driven primarily by incremental costs for the acquisition of Paradigm and incremental investment in R&D.

Net loss applicable to common shares was $9.1 million or $0.14 per fully diluted common share for the first quarter of 2019 compared to a net loss applicable to common shares of $1.9 or $0.03 per fully diluted common share in the first quarter of 2018. As outlined in the reconciliation tables presented in our earnings release, excluding the impact of the various non-recurring charges, adjusted net income applicable to common shares was $0.5 million or $0.01 per fully diluted common share in the first quarter of 2019.

Turning to guidance, based on our current business outlook, we are reiterating financial guidance for 2019. We continue to expect full-year revenues of $325 million to $335 million representing approximately 15-19% growth over the prior period. We expect full-year adjusted EBITDA to be in the range of $36 million to $40 million representing approximately 7-19% growth over the prior year.

With regard to our capital structure based on the anticipated growth of the Spine portfolio, predictable cash generation from the Sports and OEM franchises, and further efforts to reduce complexity, it is our goal to reduce debt to EBITDA to less than 4 by the end of 2019. I would note the following assumptions that underpin our guidance: relatively stable economic and market conditions and regulatory environment, ongoing positive impacts from efforts to reduce complexity and implement operational excellence initiatives, the successful integration of Paradigm Spine in a positive EBITDA contribution from the acquisition which closed on March 8th of 2019, sustained favorable reimbursement from private players, and the successful ongoing transition from map3 to ViBone. Our guidance is intended to be for the full year. However, given that everyone develops quarterly models, I'll share some thoughts on the performance drivers in the second quarter of 2019.

We currently expect revenue growth will be in the range of 11-14% predominantly driven by global contributions from the Paradigm acquisition.

Operator, I'd like to open the line to questions.

Questions and Answers:

Operator

At this time, I would like to remind everyone in order to ask a question, please press * then 1 on your telephone keypad. Pausing for just one moment to compile a Q&A roster.

Your first question comes from Matt Hewitt from Craig-Hallum Capital. Your line is open.

Matt Hewitt-- Craig-Hallum Capital Group -- Analyst

Good morning, gentlemen. Congratulations on the progress.

Camille Farhat -- President and Chief Executive Officer

Thank you.

Matt Hewitt-- Craig-Hallum Capital Group -- Analyst

I apologize if you covered this. I was slow getting onto the call. As far as Paradigm is concentered, where you are from an integration standpoint. Secondly, maybe talk a little bit about the reimbursement landscape, how things are progressing with some of those discussions and when we might be hearing about some new coverage coming on board.

Camille Farhat -- President and Chief Executive Officer

Thanks, again, for joining this morning and always the good questions coming up. Let me address both of these questions. I would say that on the integration side, it's progressing as expected. It really mirrors the activity that we have done with the integration of Zyga with one exception. We have taken advantage of Zyga and coflex together to create that novel therapies channel that is a dedicated sales force with support around that with training and the ordering aspect of that as well as the adoption curve, including reimbursement and clinical support for both of these therapies. That's the additional part that we have done compared to what we had in our playbook for the Zyga integration.

We anticipate that sometime in Q3 we will be done with the integration and then commercially that channel has been established, the structure is in place, and we have some open positions. We are filling them right now. Reimbursement, if you look at the season, this is usually the time of the year where insurance companies revisit their policies. So, we're a little bit late to make adjustments to the previous strategies. So, as an initial step, we're waiting to see the outcome of the efforts that Paradigm has put into this and they have done a lot of great work. We'll see what comes out of that. We also are in the process of setting up a reimbursement summit with a few of the experts based on what we hear on the feedback to adjust our approach if need be. That said, Matt, based on the guidance that Jon has shared earlier, we're not reliant and we don't need additional reimbursement coverage for the 2019 guidance numbers. We feel very good about where we are and the opportunity through the focus and the leverage of the dedicated sales force channel and the broader RTI established therapies channel to get us there. It would be nice to have these as they would accelerate that.

Matt Hewitt-- Craig-Hallum Capital Group -- Analyst

That's great. Thank you for that extra color on that guidance piece, there. Jon, I think this came up a couple of times in your prepared remarks. Can you just walk us through the patent litigation and what's going on there and what's being addressed?

Jonathon Singer -- Chief Financial Officer

So, we've got another not for profit tissue processor that has patents on products that look a lot like products that we've been marketing for 20 years. These are expired patents that they felt needed to be litigated. We believe we've got multiple levels of defense. Right now, we have filed IPRs on the patents because we believe that the patents themselves are not valid based on prior art. So, we filed the IPRs in January and actually got a stay of the litigation in the Federal Court of Florida mid-March. So, pretty significant costs in the preparation of the IPRs that were in the quarter. About $1.5 million incremental costs over the prior year. With the stay, we think the spending is now down to a slow simmer. The litigate has 45 days to respond and then there is a set period within the IPR process. If you look at the IPR process, which is relatively new, somewhere north of 65% of the patents that go through the process are determined to be invalid.

So, that's where we sat. It's kind of unfortunate because I don't think patients or the gift of the donor are being enhanced in any way by this activity. It's simple economics. Again, we believe we're on the right side of the law and we're going to continue to defend it.

Matt Hewitt-- Craig-Hallum Capital Group -- Analyst

Great. Thank you for those details. Maybe the last question and I'll hop off. Where does the Spine sales force sit today? Camille, you mentioned you'll be looking to add maybe a couple of resources in the back half of the year. Where does that sit today both direct versus indirect and where do you see the year exiting? Thank you.

Camille Farhat -- President and Chief Executive Officer

I just would like to clarify that we don't have a direct sales force. If you go back to what I said in my comments, the nature of novel therapies since they are not standard of care, there is a big element of demand generation, training, clinical evaluation, reimbursement, and the like that distributors are reluctant to make the investment. So, it's for us to establish that directly with the surgeon and the practice, support them as they go through that, and then after a few procedures and then turn it over to the distributor as that kind of moves forward. That's a little bit different than the established therapies where you are reliant more on what I'm going to call channel management, distributor development programs on contracting. So, both of these sales forces are within the Spine franchise. They both hold rapport. Actually, we split the marketing organization that way as well.

So, sales for novel therapies, marketing for novel therapies have their leaders. Established therapies have their leader in sales and marketing. All four report to Tom Popeck within the Spine franchise. Yes, to the point you were making. Yes, we are making investments to expand our established therapies as well. So, we feel very excited and bullish. As you know, not only did we spend last year in differentiated pipelines, but we also got the product pipeline going and we anticipate new products to be introduced in late 2019 and into 2020 in the US and internationally. Again, it gives us confidence when I look at the established therapies, let's just call it the legacy business. If you take out the map3 in part of Jon's comments, that business grew 9%. So, we feel very good that will continue to grow with more products coming. We're happy to make the investment.

Matt Hewitt-- Craig-Hallum Capital Group -- Analyst

Great. Thanks very much.

Camille Farhat -- President and Chief Executive Officer

Thank you.

Operator

Again, that's *1 if you would like to ask a question.

Your next question comes from the line of David Turkaly from JMP Securities. Your line is open.

David Turkaly--JMP Securities -- Analyst

Thanks. I apologize up front. I've been bouncing around a little too. I heard that you're splitting the Spine group into novel versus established. You just talked about separate sales forces, but will there be any cross-selling of those devices for the folks who are doing established? Can they have access to the other stuff or is it really going to be two separate units managed separately moving forward?

Camille Farhat -- President and Chief Executive Officer

They're not two separate units. It's just based on what you need in order to be effective in treating patients with the right therapies. One is the standard of care and everybody knows about them. Right? And one requires incremental focus. But truly, it's the same surgeon at the end of the day that would like to come our way and be comfortable with RTI. We just feel that with a different level of capabilities that are required with novel therapies and effort and energy, whether it is reimbursement support, whether it is patient selection, whether it is training, that allows us to establish a more direct presence and strengthen the brand with the surgeon. After a few procedures, turn that over to the distributor that hopefully carries the breadth of the product portfolio with RTI.

So, it's really more, I think, of the established therapy as more like the conductors. If that surgeon wants or is convinced about expanding their practice and having more choices to treat their patients, then we invite that specialist and the group that supports them (from reimbursement to clinical specialist, to contracting, etcetera) to support that practice along the adoption curve. Did that help a little bit, David?

David Turkaly--JMP Securities -- Analyst

Yeah. No. Thank you for that. Secondly --

Camille Farhat -- President and Chief Executive Officer

I'm sorry, David. It was different than, for example, what a ViBone is doing for that novel therapy and creating a focus and trying to move the adoption curve.

David Turkaly--JMP Securities -- Analyst

Got it. Thank you for that. Secondly, on the ViBone sort of swap out with the map3. I just was wondering if there is any color there, how that's going. Can you just remind us sort of the ASP profiles of those two products?

Camille Farhat -- President and Chief Executive Officer

Yes. So, what I will say is that ViBone is progressing in line with what we have shared with you. So, based on our numbers that are in the guidance, we are on track. We continue, as we have said to you, we think that we are not going to see the full impact until we go through the year. There was quite a bit of interest right now and people trying. Some already converted and some continue to work with the system as they put things through. Some are using this as an opportunity to reset and have a fresh look on the Biologic portfolio. We are very happy with the progress of ViBone. We are investing in that product. We will see other derivative products coming out later this year and more clinical studies that will be initiated. We already have one under way. Just like I said last time, as we built the evidence for map3, we're building the evidence around ViBone.

In terms of the pricing, David, it will be comparable to map3 and I'll have to get back to you on the exact pricing.

David Turkaly--JMP Securities -- Analyst

No worries. That's fine. A housekeeping one for Jon. What are your expected weighted shares outstanding for the year? I realize that the first quarter had a loss due to some charges but I'm just curious if you have a number, sort of what we will be looking at for the fully diluted shares post Paradigm?

Jonathon Singer -- Chief Financial Officer

Yeah. It's about $74 million is kind of the number to think about. So, you've got around numbers, 11 million shares that came in on March 8th that had a 0.17 weighting in the quarter that will be fully weighted as you get there. So, think around that number.

David Turkaly--JMP Securities -- Analyst

Great. Thanks.

Operator

There are no further questions at this time. I'll turn the call back to our hosts.

Camille Farhat -- President and Chief Executive Officer

Okay. I want to thank everybody for the ongoing interest in RTI Surgical Holdings. We are confident that our strategic transformation continues to build momentum and we are creating an organization with significant long-term value. We are focused on the execution of our plans and we look forward to updating you on our on-going progress. Thanks for joining and juggling your schedule to be with us. Have a terrific day.

Operator

Ladies and gentlemen, thank you for your participation. This concludes today's conference call and you may now disconnect.

Duration: 30 minutes
