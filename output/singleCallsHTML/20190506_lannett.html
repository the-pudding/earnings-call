Aspirin and Extend-Release Dipyridamole Capsules a more complex combination and extended release product from our internal development efforts has been approved and is expected to launch this summer.

Through the first three quarters of fiscal 2019, sales of our unapproved C-Topical have averaged approximately $3 million a quarter. As such we do not believe the discontinuation will have a significant impact on our future operating results and in any event, we had anticipated the eventual withdrawal of this product. We plan to be back in that market subsequent to receiving an FDA approval of the NDA, hopefully around the end of the calendar year.

Last quarter, we reported that a clinical trial application was filed with the South African authorities. Looking forward, we currently expect to complete the dosing of healthy adult males in a PK, PD study by September of this year. And of course, our business development and commercial teams continue to evaluate a pipeline of transactions to expand our portfolio. Our USA generic focus free of the burden of global fragmentation seen in some of our peers along with a solid end market product performances has made us a partner of choice for several firms. Since January of this year, we had completed several transactions for several products a couple of which have not yet been announced, pending clarification of operational readiness or other competitive reasoning.

To sum up, excluding Levothyroxine our product sales have been growing fueled by the launch of new products while maintaining our base business. We are on track to realize $33 million of net cost savings savings, which is starting to be reflected in our results.

Given our size, we have a significant pipeline of products that includes internally developed as well as in-licensed products.
