Freight remained a drag, though not to the same degree we experienced through 2018. Our net rebates were a slight negative as well as a result of efforts to limit inventory growth.

We expect to make further progress toward eliminating this deficit in the second quarter of 2019 and over the course of the full year.

Our operating margin was 20% in the first quarter of 2019, up 20 basis points year-over-year. Occupancy-related costs were up 2.3%, generating 35 basis points of leverage. We generated 20 basis points of leverage over other operating administrative expenses.

As described in the past successfully, our growth drivers is likely to reduce gross margin over time, but also provides the platform and volume that generates good operating expense leverage. We continue to experience inflationary pressure on our inventories. However, during the period, we worked off the foreign sourced inventory that was accelerated into the US in the fourth quarter of 2018 and advanced several initiatives aimed at making us more efficient with inventory.

AR grew 15.2% in the first quarter of 2019, with customers continuing to aggressively push payments out past quarter end. As has been the case in past quarters, we are not seeing any meaningful change in hard-to-collect balances.
