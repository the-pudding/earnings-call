Thank you Crystal, and thank you everyone for being with us today. With that, I'll now turn the call over to Dave Demski our CEO.

David M. Demski -- Chief Executive Officer

Thank you Brian and good afternoon everyone. I'm very pleased to announce record revenues of $196 million for the fourth quarter, an increase of 11.3% over Q4 '17 and our fifth consecutive quarter of double-digit organic growth. Non-GAAP EPS was also record $0.45 per share, an increase of 18% over last year. For the full year, revenue came in at $713 million an increase of 12% and non-GAAP EPS was $1.67 per share, up 28% over 2017.

Our U.S. Spine business continue to accelerate growing by 9.4% over the fourth quarter of last year. Our U.S. business grew by 5.9% for the full year well above the overall market and we finished the year with strong underlying momentum, as the second half of the year grew at 8. 5%. Implant pull through from ExcelsiusGPS placements and back-to-back record sales force recruiting years were the primary growth drivers. We remain intensely focused on both of these areas going into 2019, which when combined with a robust line up of anticipated product launches bodes well for continued growth.

Our international spine business grew by 11.7% in Q4 and 13.7% on a constant-currency basis. Our business in Japan continued its strong growth in Q4. The business in Spain turned around significantly during the quarter. Italy continued its outstanding performance and we saw strength in Brazil. For the full year, our international business grew by 9.1% as reported and 7.6% on a constant currency basis led by Japan, Italy, Australia, Germany and Brazil. We are optimistic going into 2019 that our international spine business is poised to grow at rates in excess of the U.S. spine business.

For the fourth quarter, revenue from our ExcelsiusGPS system was $14.3 million. This is an increase of 31% over the fourth quarter of last year, which is particularly significant considering the pent-up demand reflected in last year's number caused by the initial delay in our launch. We remain extremely bullish on the potential of computer-assisted surgery, navigation, robotics and imaging to transform the ways spine surgery is done. We are highly encouraged by the level of adoption across our installed base. This is a key area of focusing investment for us, as the value experienced by early adaptors is essential to driving the next wave of buyers.

We continue to win the battle in a vast majority of head-to-head technology evaluations. We are making great progress toward product enhancements and we remain flexible and responsive to our customer's capital constraints by offering tailored business solutions. The nascent nature of this market combined with our strong balance sheet enables us to compete effectively with competitors, who are much larger than we are.

We made further progress in our trauma business during the quarter with our focus continuing to be the production of inventory to support the national roll-out of all core systems. While the pace is not where we wanted to be, we have expanded our internal and external production capacity and have greatly increased our presence in many markets. We continue to invest heavily in terms of sets and sales force expenditures and remain fully committed to becoming a technology leader in this space.

2018 was an excellent year for Globus. Not only did we achieve record revenue and EPS, our organic sales growth was the highest it has been since we've been a public company and our profitability and cash flow continue to be the envy of the industry. The promise of our entering into the world robotic surgery has been validated by our strong performance and leadership position. As we enter 2019, we stand on the cusp of a very bright future driven by a passion for innovation across all of our businesses.

I'll now turn the call over to Dan.

Daniel T. Scavilla -- Senior Vice President and Chief Financial Officer

Thanks Dave, and good afternoon everyone. Globus delivered strong financial results in Q4 with continued above-market gains in the U.S. business, accelerated growth in international markets and record placements of ExcelsiusGPS systems. Full year revenue was $713 million growing 12.1% as reported with one more selling day in 2018.

GAAP net income was $156.5 million and non-GAAP net income was $169.4 million both driven by strong operational growth, the full year effect of the U.S. tax reform and one-time tax benefits. Fully diluted non-GAAP earnings per share were $1.67 with an adjusted EBITDA of 34.5% and $121.9 million of free cash flow. Q4 revenue was $195.9 million, growing at 11.3% as reported, or 11.6% in constant currency with one more selling day in the quarter versus prior year.

GAAP net income was $36.8 million and non-GAAP net income was $44.1 million, delivering $0.43 fully diluted non-GAAP earnings per share and adjusted EBITDA of 34.1% and $27.1 million of free cash flow.

Focusing on sales, U.S. revenue for the quarter was $163.8 million, 10.7% higher than Q4 '17. The growth was derived from strong performance in implant sales driven by competitive on-boarding, increased implant pull-through from accounts with our robot and continued placement of ExcelsiusGPS systems. International revenue for the quarter was $32.2 million growing 14.7% as reported, or 16. 8% in constant currency. Gains are achieved through continued market penetration in Japan, Spain, Italy and other key markets coupled with ExcelsiusGPS sales.

Turning to the rest of the P& L. Q4 gross profit was 76.5% compared to 76.8% in Q4 '17. The change is primarily due to the planned increase and appreciation for cases and instruments, as we expand our sales forces in spine, trauma, and robotics globally. Full year gross profit remains strong at 77.6% and full year 2019 gross profit is projected to be in the mid-70s.

Research and development expenses for the fourth quarter were $13.8 million, or 7% of sales compared to $11.4 million, or 6.5% in Q4 '17, resulting from increased investments in the INR platform.

SG&A expenses for the fourth quarter were $83.6 million, or 42.7% compared to $73 million, or 41.4% in Q4 '17, reflecting our planned reinvestment of the U.S. tax reform savings to drive future growth. Expanding the U.S. sales force in spine, building out the robotic and trauma commercial teams, growing the Japanese sales force and increasing surgeon education are the primary investment areas.

The GAAP income tax rate for Q4 was 22.8% compared to 51.8% in Q4 '17. The current period reflects the U.S. tax reform reduced federal rates, while Q4 2017 included a one-time $11 million tax reform charge.

Full year 2018 GAAP income tax rate was 17%, which included 440 basis points of one-time benefits related to stock option exercises and manufacturing credits that are not planned to repeat in 2019. We project an effective tax rate for 2019 of approximately 21%. GAAP fourth quarter net income was $36.8 million and non-GAAP net income was $44.1 million.

GAAP diluted earnings per share were $0.36 and non-GAAP diluted earnings per share were $0.43, growing 15.1%, including continued investments in emerging technologies that negatively impacted Q4 '18 EPS by $0.04. Full year non-GAAP earnings per share were $1.67, which include $0.08 of one-time tax benefits for stock option exercises and manufacturing credits. Adjusted EBITDA margin for Q4 2018 was 34.1% and full year EBITDA margin was 34.5%.

The company continues to deliver best-in-class margins and profitability, while investing for the future growth. In 2018, we drove meaningful investments in sales force expansion for spine and robotics globally, expanded the trauma sales force, increased INR development program investments, expanded manufacturing capabilities and created broader surgeon reach through education programs.

Full year 2019 EBITDA margin is projected to be in the mid-30s. We ended the quarter with $602.8 million of cash, cash equivalents and marketable securities. Net cash provided by operating activities in Q4 was $44.3 million and free cash flow was $27.1 million. The company remains debt free.

The company reaffirms guidance for full year 2019 sales of $770 million and non-GAAP diluted earnings per share of $1.72. Implied revenue growth of 2018 is 8%. Normalizing the 2018 non-GAAP diluted EPS to $1.59 by reflecting the $0.08 of one-time tax benefits for stock option exercises and manufacturing credits, resulted in implied operational growth in non-GAAP diluted EPS of 8.2% in line with revenue guidance.
