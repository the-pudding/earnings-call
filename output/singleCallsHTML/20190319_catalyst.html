Prior to the approval of Firdapse, these patients had no evidence-based treatment option that was approved for use. Furthermore, only a small number of LEMS patients had access to any treatment at all in the form of an unapproved investigational amifampridine treatment.

I'd like to focus my remarks today on our three strategic priorities. First, and most importantly, the successful execution of the Firdapse launch plan. And third, our efforts to develop a longer acting formulation of Firdapse.

I'd like to start by informing you all about what has been a very strong start to the commercial launch of Firdapse. As most of you know, we were launch ready as of January 15th. As part of that effort, we have successfully transitioning patients from the Cleveland Clinic, Duke University, the Mayo Clinic, as well as other centers of excellence. During this transition, I'm pleased to report that so far we are not aware of any patient from an early access program desiring to remain on therapy that has missed a single dose of drug.

Dan will provide more details on our commercial activities shortly.

With the MuSK-MG trial we are working with some of the top neuromuscular experts at 29 clinical trial sites in the US and Europe. These programs continue to enroll and we anticipate top line results for the MuSK-MG and CMS trials in the second half of this year.

Our third priority is moving forward on our development efforts for a long acting formulation of Firdapse, that addresses additional important LEMS patient needs. Patients have told us that they would like a product that provides relief from LEMS symptoms throughout the day, so they don't have to plan their daily activities around their schedule dosing times.

They've also expressed a desire and need for LEMS medication that last through the night while they sleep. Also, we have received patient request for a formulation that could be dosed once or twice each day, which would provide a steadier state of drug effect throughout the day and likely lead to better medication, adherence and compliance. We're designing our Firdapse sustain release program specifically to address these patient needs and based on their input. It is gratifying to know that through this development program, we may be able to further improve the lives of LEMS patients.

Firdapse and its active ingredient are made in the United States. We use two different manufacturers of Firdapse in order to ensure that unforeseen interruptions to manufacturing will not affect the availability of medications.
