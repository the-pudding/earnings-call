Carpenter Technology Corp (CRS) Q1 2019 Earnings Conference Call Transcript

Carpenter Technology Corp  (NYSE:CRS)
Q1 2019 Earnings Conference Call
Oct. 24, 2018, 10:00 a.m. ET

Contents:

  • Questions and Answers
  • Call Participants

Prepared Remarks:

Operator

Good day, and welcome to the Carpenter Technology Corporation First Quarter Fiscal Year 2019 Conference Call. All participants will be in listen-only mode. (Operator Instructions) After today's presentation there will be an opportunity to ask questions. (Operator Instructions) Please note, this event is being recorded.

I would now like to turn the conference over to Mr. Brad Edwards, Investor Relations. Mr. Edwards, the floor is yours sir.

Brad Edwards -- Investor Relations

Thank you, operator. Good morning, everyone, and welcome to Carpenter's earnings conference call for the first quarter ended September 30th, 2018. This call is also being broadcast over the Internet along with presentation slides. Please note, for those of you listening by phone, you may experience a time delay in slide movement. Speakers on the call today are Tony Thene, President and Chief Executive Officer; and Tim Lain, Vice President and Chief Financial Officer.

Statements made by management during this earnings presentation that are forward-looking statements are based on current expectations. Risk factors that could cause actual results to differ materially from those forward-looking statements can be found in Carpenter's most recent SEC filings, including the company's report on Form 10-K for the year ended June 30th, 2018, and the exhibits attached to that filing. Please also note that in the following discussion, unless otherwise noted, when management discusses sales or revenue, that reference excludes surcharge. When referring to operating margins that is based on operating income and sales excluding surcharge.

I will now turn the call over to Tony.

Tony Thene -- Chief Executive Officer

Thank you, Brad, and good morning to everyone on the call today. Let's begin with an update of our safety performance on Slide 4. Our total case incident rate or TCIR was 1.7 in the first quarter fiscal year 2019, up from 1.1 for the full fiscal year 2018. Our PEP business has continued to make exceptional progress toward a zero injury workplace with only one recordable injury in the first quarter. However, our SAO business took a step back in terms of safety performance. The results are unacceptable and our safety teams are using this as an opportunity to increase the level of engagement with employees and to develop and execute action plans to get us back on track. It's clear to me that every injury to date could have been prevented and we must regain the uninterrupted focus on hazard elimination and standardized work. As I've stated in the past and I'll say again today, we will not be satisfied with our safety performance until we achieve a core value of a zero injury workplace.

Now let's turn to Slide 5, and review the first quarter. Our first quarter results reflect the ongoing execution of our solutions-focused strategy. Strong demand patterns across to our end-use markets and further market share gains. In terms of earnings, it was the best first quarter since fiscal year 2014. However, we did fall a bit short of our expectations that were communicated in last quarter's earnings call. I will address that now. In the SAO segment, we completed our planned annual preventive maintenance outage. With the robust market demand that we are experiencing, our equipment is running at high utilization rates, and it is vital that we take critical operations offline to perform necessary, preventative, maintenance to ensure that the equipment continues to operate at its maximum capability. The average was extended its certain production centers to ensure we had completed all required tasks sufficiently. The extension of the outage account for the majority of the deviation from our quarterly guidance. In addition, certain input prices were higher than originally expected.

In our PEP segment, we were short on our expectations mainly as a result of our Dynamet and Amega West businesses. At Dynamet, the lingering operational impact of the fire recovery and inconsistency in the fastener demand drove lower than expected results. For Amega West, this quarters' results were below our expectations mostly due to the timing of certain rental tool arrangements. We remain encouraged by the activity levels at Amega West, especially with our customers in the Permian Basin and are encouraged by early signs is a growth in activity outside the US. The fundamentals of both Dynamet and Amega West remains solid, supported by robust end-use markets and customer demand. In summary, our operations continue to perform well, as we took the necessary preventative maintenance shutdowns to preserve our capabilities going forward, all while still generating the best Q1 earnings, since fiscal year 2014.

For this quarter's earnings call, we have four significant points to emphasize; continued market strength, increased customer urgency on Athens Qualifications, Carpenter's acquisition of LPW Technology which builds on our leading position in the additive manufacturing industry, and our strong balance sheet leading to direct returns to our shareholders. Let me speak briefly about each one now and then get into more detail later in the presentation. Demand conditions across our markets remain strong and we generated healthy year-over-year sales growth in each of our five end-use markets during the quarter. In addition, backlogs continue to grow, up 38% compared to last year and up 9% sequentially. This quarter marks the ninth consecutive quarter of sequential backlog growth. Drilling down specifically to the Aerospace and Defense end-use market, sales increased 11% compared to last year, driven by engine sub-market activity remaining at near record levels and the benefit of our diverse industry participation. The first quarter marked the seventh consecutive quarter of year-over-year sales growth in our Aerospace and Defense end-use market.

Another consistent bright spot has been the medical end-use market, where sales were up 18% compared to last year. Our growth in this market is outpacing industry growth projections, due to robust demand for our leading titanium solutions, as well as the success we've had in expanding our customer relationships. A significant earnings growth enabler for Carpenter is our Athens facility. This quarter we experienced a noticable increase in customer engagement concerning qualification of their specific engine products. Our customers recognized the urgency to qualify additional capacity given increase in lead times and the projected airframe and engine build rates. Our technical teams are hosting on-site visit with large OEMs and are actively engaged with customer contacts to exchange information toward completing each of their qualifications.

This morning, we announced the acquisition of LPW Technology, a leading global developer and supplier of metal powder solutions for additive manufacturing, including software and hardware-based powder lifecycle management solutions. The acquisition of LPW adds an innovative solution to managing powder quality during a 3D printing process and improving powder recyclability to our existing additive manufacturing platform. This acquisition keeps Carpenter at the forefront of the additive manufacturing industry, and we continue to build out an impressive group of assets and capabilities. Lastly, our solid balance sheet gives us the important flexibility to invest for the future and to provide increased direct returns to our shareholders. To that end, during the first quarter, we increased our regular quarterly cash dividend by 11%, which reflects our solid financial position as well as our confidence in our ability to generate sustainable long-term growth.

Let's move to Slide 6, in the end-use market update. I'll begin with Aerospace and Defense, where sales were up 11% on a year-over-year basis, driven primarily by continued strong demand in the engine sub-market, as well as higher avionics and distribution sales. Our total Aerospace and Defense backlog continues to increase and was up 40% on a year-over-year basis and 12% sequentially. Year-over-year, we experienced backlog growth in each of our sub-markets. In the engine sub-market, we achieved another strong quarter with sales up 17% compared to last year, as market demand remains robust given the new platform ramp. We also continue to fulfill expedited orders for our OEM partners given current engine build rates and industry lead times.

The fastener sub-market sales were down both year-over-year and sequentially, as visibility remains limited with inconsistent demand patterns. While the fastener's near-term outlook is hard to predict, we are confident in our market position given our diverse offerings and believe the long-term demand levels are supported by the backlog of over 14,000 aircraft. In the structural sub-market, we continue to experience healthy demand for solutions used on major platforms including the A320 and the 737. Finally, sales in our Defense sub-market increased both year-over-year and sequentially due to program-specific demand.

Now moving to the energy market and our oil and gas and power generation sub-markets. Total energy sales were up 30% year-over-year, due to higher oil and gas sub-market sales and higher rental and replacement activity at Amega West. The North American directional and horizontal rig count has largely stabilized in the range of 1,100 rigs and we see further opportunities particularly in the Permian Basin with drilling activity by our customers remained at healthy levels. In the power generation sub-market, overall demand and order levels remained at relatively low baseline.

Now, onto the transportation market, where sales were up 3% year-over-year, as a rebound in the heavy truck market matures. In this market, we have been making solid progress expanding into attractive market adjacencies and diversifying our revenue streams. Sales into those additional sub-markets such as heavy-duty off-road, marine and rail were up over 50% compared to the first quarter of last year. While our selective push into these sub-markets remained in the early stages, I'm pleased with our progress so far and believe we have additional growth opportunities in these areas.

Moving onto the Medical market, sales were up 18% compared to last year, due to stronger demand for our titanium solutions at Dynamet. Our Medical portfolio has been an area of strategic emphasis over the last several years. We've worked hard to balance our traditional customer base with more direct OEM relationships, while diligently implementing the Carpenter Operating Model to unlock incremental capacity to meet rising customer demand. Today, we are recognized in this market for both our leading portfolio of material and processing solutions and our commitment to advance solutions, including additive manufacturing. In the industrial and consumer end-use market, revenues were up 4% on a year-over-year basis, due to increased sales in certain sub-markets including semiconductors as well as sporting goods.

Now, I'll turn it over to Tim for the financial review.

Tim Lain -- Vice President and Chief Financial Officer

Thank you, Tony. Good morning everyone. I'll start on Slide 8, the income statement summary. Our first quarter results represent a strong start to our fiscal year. In fact, as Tony mentioned, this quarter's results mark the strongest first quarter operating income in five years. Net sales in the first quarter were $572 million, or $456 million excluding surcharge. Sales excluding surcharge decreased by 8% on a sequential basis on 10% lower volume. Historically, our first quarter sales relative to the preceding fourth quarter decline due to the timing of our planned annual preventative maintenance shutdowns at key work centers across our facilities. We continue to see signs of strength in our business, including increased demand across most of our end-use markets, as we execute on our solutions-focused strategy.

From the first quarter a year ago, sales were up 11% on 4% higher volumes. In addition, our backlog continues to grow. Most notably in Aerospace and Defense with year-over-year backlog growth of 40% and sequential growth of 12%. SG&A expenses declined by $8.3 million sequentially to $47 million. The reduction is mostly attributable to the timing of certain costs in Q4, that did not repeat in Q1. Year-over-year SG&A expenses increased by $3.2 million, due mostly to targeted investments in key areas. As an administrative note given the current adoption of a recent accounting standard, operating income and the reporting we previously made related to operating income excluding pension EID are now one in the same and will simply be referred to as operating income. Prior period numbers have been recast to conform to the current year presentation.

As a percent of sales, operating income was 9.9% in the quarter. Our margin declined as expected on a sequential basis compared to 12.1% in the fourth quarter, due to lower volume. Operating margin declined 40 basis points as compared to the first quarter of fiscal year 2018. In addition, our operating margin was also negatively impacted by ongoing fire recovery efforts at our Dynamet business, which we will discuss in more detail in a moment. Our effective tax rate for the first quarter was 21.8%, which reflects certain discreet tax benefits associated with stock-based compensation, equity awards vesting and exercises in the current quarter. We continue to expect our effective tax rate to be in the range of 24% to 26% for the balance of fiscal year 2019. First quarter net income was $31.5 million, or $0.65 per share. Year-over-year EPS grew 33%.

Now turning to Slide 9, in a review of free cash flow. Free cash flow in the first quarter was negative $42 million, compared to positive $56 million in the fourth quarter of fiscal year 2018. This performance was consistent with our historical sequential declines as the fourth quarter is generally our strongest free cash flow quarter in each year. We generally build inventory in the first half of the fiscal year to support the higher sales volumes in the second half of our fiscal year. To that end, we increased inventory by $51 million during the current quarter, as we align the inventory levels with demand patterns we are experiencing across our end-use markets and our increasing backlog. On a year-over-year basis, free cash flow was relatively flat with higher cash from operations, mainly as a result of increased earnings and improving working capital offset by higher capital expenditures in the current period.

Capital expenditures in the first quarter of fiscal year 2019 were $41 million. We continue to expect capital expenditures for the full year to be about $190 million, as we talked about last quarter, the elevated CapEx levels relative to historical rates, reflect our strategic investments in growth projects associated with additive manufacturing and soft magnetics. Free cash flow on the quarter also reflects the impact of the increase in our quarterly cash dividend. The dividend increase is another signal of the confidence we have in the strength of our business, as well as our commitment to delivering direct returns to our shareholders. At September 30th, we had $411 million of total liquidity, including $17 million of cash and $394 million of availability under our credit facility. Our solid financial position is supported by no debt maturities, or major required pension contributions until fiscal year 2022. We remain well positioned to continue investing in targeted growth areas and strengthening our foundation for long-term sustainable growth.

Now turning to Slide 10, and an update on our SAO segment. Net sales excluding surcharge were $362 million, which was down $34 million, or 9% on a sequential basis, on a 11% lower shipment volume. Again, the sequential decline reflects the seasonal impact of our planned annual preventative maintenance during the first fiscal quarter. On a year-over-year basis, sales excluding surcharge increased $36 million, or 11%, on 2% higher shipment volume. The increase reflects continuing strong demand patterns across most of our end-use markets, driven by Aerospace and Defense and Medical combined with an improving product mix.

SAO operating income in the current quarter was $52.8 million, down $21 million compared to the fourth quarter, and up $2 million year-over-year. The sequential decline in operating income reflects the reduced shipment volumes and higher input prices on certain production elements. Operating margin was 14.6%, compared to 18.7% in the fourth quarter of last year, and 15.5% on last year's first quarter. Looking ahead, demand across our end-use markets remain strong and we are continuing to drive backlog growth. We are confident, we will continue to expand customer relationships and capitalize on emerging market opportunities. Given the number of production days available in Q2, we currently estimate that operating income for SAO will increase up to 10% sequentially from Q1 to Q2, which would represent the best second quarter operating income performance in six years.

Now turning to Slide 11, and a review of our PEP segment results. PEP net sales excluding surcharge were $108 million, down $6 million sequentially, or 5%. On a year-over-year basis, PEP sales excluding surcharge increased $8 million, or 7% driven by continued growth in our high-value titanium solutions for the Medical market and increased demand at Amega West. Operating income in the first quarter was $7.3 million, down 600,000 compared to the fourth quarter, and up $2 million year-over-year. As Tony mentioned earlier, the PEP results in the current quarter were below our expectations, as a result of our Dynamet and Amega West business units. To reiterate our Dynamet business continues to deal with short-term headwinds from a fire at one of its facilities earlier this calendar year.

Operating income continues to be impacted by sub-optimal production flow path. Reconstruction of the damage facility is well under way and it is expected to be completed over the next several months. In addition, Dynamet continues to deal with inconsistencies in fastener demand patterns, which negatively impacted results this quarter relative to our expectations. At Amega West, the current quarter's results were lower than our expectations, due to the timing of certain rental tool arrangements. Looking at the second quarter of fiscal year 2019, we currently expect PEP's operating income excluding any impacts of our recent LPW acquisition to improve sequentially by up to 10%.

With that, I will turn the call back over to Tony.

Tony Thene -- Chief Executive Officer

Thanks, Tim. Over the past several years, we have focused on expanding our capabilities in the additive manufacturing space and evolving our platform to ensure we are positioned at the forefront of this rapidly changing and disruptive technology. We strengthened our powder capabilities by acquiring Titanium Powder Producer Puris. And then last fiscal year acquired CalRAM, which brought extensive additive manufacturing product design and part production capabilities. In addition, last fiscal year, we announced our investment in an Emerging Technology Center in Athens, Alabama, which will enhance our technical capabilities and scale. We've also mobilized over 30 engineers, scientists, operators and technicians, who specialized specifically in additive manufacturing technology. Having this expertise from free (ph) stock through design and component production positions Carpenter as an irreplaceable technical partner with our customers.

During our last call, I talked about our collaboration with one of the major US oil field service providers to develop a unique 3D design and printed in-hole tool for the drill string. Overall, collaborations like this one are steadily increasing, as a growing number of customers are engaging with Carpenter Technology, due to our end-to-end additive manufacturing solution. Additive manufacturing technology maturation is now presenting commercial opportunities in advanced applications such as jet engine components, satellite structural, downhole oil and gas drilling systems, surgical instruments, medical implants and state-of-the-art defense platforms. And today, we took another meaningful step-forward with the acquisition of LPW Technology, to enhance the value proposition we offer customers in the additive manufacturing market and to build on our leading position in this space.

The acquisition of LPW Technology strengthens our additive manufacturing platform through the addition of innovative and tightly controlled powder design, material optimization and lifecycle management solutions throughout the additive manufacturing process. LPW addresses a critical need in the metals additive manufacturing marketplace by enabling quality assurance and traceability of metal powders. This is achieved by linking the physical powder products to a digital platform, which collects real-time data from sensors and other hardware, starting from the powder development phase through the printing process and multiple cycles of powder reuse. As an example, the software can accurately measure moisture and oxygen levels at different points, since variations beyond certain thresholds can lead to compromise part integrity, sub-par performance or failed quality.

LPW solutions can help identify and resolve problems before they happen.

Another major challenge facing additive manufacturing today is, powder reuse. LPW's platform is aimed at improving the lifecycle cost of powder and achieving significant cost savings by minimizing degradation issues and enabling repeat uses of metal grade powders. LPW's approach to data collection and digital manufacturing assists in making it easy to track and analyze the alloy powders leading to more rapid innovation, product enhancement and overall part integrity. These lifecycle management tools can increase the probability of achieving 3D part printing success earlier in the development process. From a customer standpoint, LPW has established relationships across many major industries, including Aerospace, Medical, Transportation and Energy. We're excited about the LPW acquisition, as it accelerates Carpenter's leadership position in the emerging additive manufacturing industry with an integrated end-to-end solution.

Let's turn to Slide 14, my closing comments. In closing, we are making continued progress in strengthening our long-term growth potential by executing our commercial strategy at a very high level. Broadening our customer relationships and investing strategically in targeted growth areas. Our first quarter results included a number of important highlights. We were successful in completing a critical, preventative maintenance outage that will allow us to continue to serve a robust market, while delivering year-over-year earnings growth. We capitalized on strong demand trends and generated year-over-year revenue growth across all of our end-use markets. And we benefited from a continued strong market and our solutions approach is driving further backlog growth, up 38% compared to last year and 9% sequentially. Our facility in Athens, Alabama remains a major growth driver with fully qualified and will offer significant incremental capacity for the aerospace industry during this major up cycle. As I noted earlier, our dialog with customers is accelerating and we are seeing increasing urgency to quantify their major aircraft components.

The future of our industry also offers significant growth opportunities for Carpenter. We know that industries and markets evolve and with that change comes new technologies as well as enhanced customer needs. We have spent the last several years transforming Carpenter into a complete solutions provider. That transformation must continue in the years ahead, as we make the strategic investments needed to remain at the forefront of disruptive change impacting our customers and our end-use market. This includes additive manufacturing with the addition of LPW's leading power lifecycle management solution, we have built a platform that offers materials and metallurgical processing expertise, powder development and optimization, part design and production capabilities, a technology-based platform that manages power during the 3D printing process and the solution to improve powder reuse and recyclability . Our future growth drivers also include our high value soft magnetic portfolio, where we are investing to significantly increase our capabilities and capacity.

Today, we are the market leader in auxiliary power use for Aerospace and see strong future growth potential given the current Aerospace cycle and projected build rates. We also see attractive future growth opportunities for our soft magnetic solutions in consumer electronics and electric vehicle applications. Our solutions-focused strategy is working and delivering solid performance in a high demand environment and we have multiple long-term growth drivers that position Carpenter for sustainable growth in the years ahead. With all of the mentioned progress, we have continued to maintain a solid balance sheet, that gives us the important flexibility to continue investing in the future and provide increased direct returns to our shareholders, as evidenced by the 11% increase in our regular quarterly cash dividend.

In summary, we have just presented a positive outlook for the future. We project our SAO business earnings to be up as much as 17% on a year-over-year basis next quarter. With PEP earnings projected to increase by as much as 7% on a year-over-year basis next quarter. We see no indications of market weakness, in fact the markets are strengthening, especially for Aerospace as backlogs and lead times continue to increase and expedited orders become more common. Our Athens facility is the only new capacity coming into the market and qualification activity has increased significantly over the last quarter. The Carpenter operating model has delivered meaningful results, but future opportunities can be significant as we continue to implement, and we continue to make strategic investments in exciting growth areas such as soft magnetics and additive manufacturing. The Carpenter team is committed to this journey and excited about the near and long-term future.

Thank you for your attention. And I'll turn it back to the operator to field your questions.

Well, Jeff, this was primarily due to the outage, our decision in some areas to extend that outage, demand was up across all of our end-use markets, backlog was up.
