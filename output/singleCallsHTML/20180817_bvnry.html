For more variable assays, you often see fourfold. But in our assays and as supported by KOLs that have looked at the data and other partners, potential partners that are looking at the data, these are significant increases in serum antibodies. When you start looking at the mucosal responses, again, that's why we always stratify it to show the weaker and the stronger immunity. We are seeing more marginal fold increases, but you do see those increases in those with weaker immunity, which is lending to a more convincing data that the increases we're seeing are in-- are real and significant in terms of what we're allowed-- what we can actually boost.

In terms of future considerations, as I said, we haven't openly talked about what we believe Phase 3 requirements will be because we have our view and opinion. But until we actually discuss them with the FDA, I don't see the point in doing that. But we have a number of different options that we wish to discuss with the FDA. So hopefully, during the second half of this month, we should get-- second half of this year, we should get much greater clarity on what the Phase 3 requirements will be.

Paul Chaplin -- President and Chief Executive Officer

So, it's very few subjects that are actually enrolled and is primarily looking at the safety of either CV301 alone or in combination with nivo, which was our initial start of the study over KEYTRUDA. So, it's primarily a safety study. The sort of data that we will be reporting on is, obviously, safety immunogenicity.

In that CV301, as you know, we changed the platform from PROSTVAC. We re-engineered the priming dose to be an MVA-based, which we give at very high dose levels to each limb. So, there are four vaccinations, one in two arms and the other in the two legs. And we do that four weeks apart.

So, we're giving a lot more MVA or poxvirus than we did with PROSTVAC, and then we follow up with fowlpox boosters. So, there will be immunogenicity data, which will be important for CV301 as a platform. And then potentially, there are objective responses from the patients at the turn of the year. And I think the real read-through will be the immune responses. Are we actually stimulating good T cells to [Inaudible]. And then the real-- so the main reason for not continuing into Phase 2, we said all along from 2014 that our strategy for CV301 was to look at three indications with checkpoint blockade.
